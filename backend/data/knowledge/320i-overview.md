## BMW 320i (demo overview)

The 320i is often used in coursework examples as a mid-size saloon that balances commuting comfort with a premium feel. Demo pricing and performance figures in this repository are placeholders.

### Who it might suit

Buyers who want a conventional saloon shape, predictable refuelling, and widespread dealer/specialist support may lean towards petrol models. If you mostly drive long distances without charging infrastructure, petrol can be simpler operationally.

### Running cost angles (demo)

Fuel economy varies by driving style and traffic. Urban stop-start cycles tend to reduce efficiency. Motorway cruising can be more efficient per mile, but average speed and aerodynamic drag still matter.

### Options and trims (generic)

Equipment packages can change headlights, seats, driver assistance features, and infotainment. For academic work, treat "trim level" as a configuration problem: two cars with the same badge can differ materially.

### Maintenance mindset

Regular oil changes and inspections matter for long-term reliability perceptions. A well-documented history can support resale value arguments in a dissertation discussion.

### Disclaimer

Do not treat this demo text as specifications for any real market year.
