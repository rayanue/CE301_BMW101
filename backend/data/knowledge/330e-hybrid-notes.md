## BMW 330e plug-in hybrid notes (demo)

Plug-in hybrids attempt to combine electric driving for short trips with an combustion engine for longer distances. The usefulness of a PHEV depends heavily on whether you can charge regularly.

### When a PHEV tends to make sense

If your daily commute is short and you can charge at home or work, electric miles can reduce fuel spend. If you rarely charge, a PHEV may behave more like a heavier petrol car, which can reduce the economic advantage.

### Battery and usability questions (demo)

Ask about battery warranty period (as a concept), whether the seller includes a charging cable, and whether the vehicle shows consistent electric mode behaviour during a test drive.

### Servicing (high level)

PHEVs still need engine servicing in addition to electrical system checks. Some maintenance items align with petrol cars, while others relate to high-voltage systems and cooling circuits.

### Academic framing

You can evaluate PHEVs using a simple rubric: charging access, electric range adequacy, long-trip efficiency, and complexity tolerance.
