## BMW i4 (demo overview)

The i4 is commonly described as a gran coupe-style electric BMW: a sleeker silhouette than a typical SUV, often appealing to buyers who want a sportier look while retaining rear doors for practicality.

### Driving character (conceptual)

Sport-oriented trims often emphasise firmer suspension tuning and stronger acceleration. Comfort-oriented configurations may prioritise ride compliance and noise isolation. In coursework, you can treat these as design trade-offs rather than absolutes.

### Cabin and usability (demo)

Rear headroom and boot access can be affected by roofline styling. If you frequently carry tall passengers or bulky luggage, a checklist approach helps: measure boot opening height and compare against your typical cargo.

### EV planning (demo)

Long trips benefit from a charging plan: identify reliable charging operators along the route and build buffer for queues, slower-than-expected sessions, or detours.

### Comparison hooks

In tutorials, i4 is often compared conceptually to other premium EV saloons/coupes. Keep comparisons grounded in buyer needs: charging access, rear space, ride comfort, and running costs.
