## i4 vs iX1: a buyer-oriented comparison (demo)

This comparison is intentionally practical. It is not an official BMW comparison sheet.

### Body style and daily ergonomics

The iX1 sits higher and can feel more "SUV-like" for visibility and step-in height. The i4 sits lower and can feel more car-like, which some drivers prefer for motorway stability sensations (tyres and suspension tuning matter a lot here).

### Passenger and luggage priorities

If rear-seat headroom is a frequent concern, a higher-roof SUV form factor can be easier for some passengers. If your priority is sleek styling and a more coupe-like silhouette, a lower-roof vehicle may match aesthetics better at the cost of packaging compromises.

### EV usage patterns

Both vehicles require charging planning if you lack home charging. The more predictable your weekly mileage, the easier budgeting becomes. If your mileage is irregular, public charging reliance increases.

### Cost framing (demo)

Compare insurance groups, expected tyre sizes, and servicing plans. Premium vehicles can have higher parts costs even when powertrains are simpler, because brakes, tyres, and suspension components may be performance-oriented.
