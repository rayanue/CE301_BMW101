## iX1 charging notes (demo)

Charging an electric vehicle is a workflow, not a single number. Many owners establish a routine: overnight charging at home, occasional public charging for longer trips, and faster charging only when needed.

### AC vs DC charging (conceptual)

AC charging is commonly used at home or at destinations where the car's onboard equipment converts AC to stored energy. DC rapid charging can add energy faster but may be more expensive per kWh and can depend heavily on charger power, battery temperature, and state of charge curves.

For demos, explain that "minutes to 80%" marketing figures often omit real-world variability.

### Cold weather and efficiency (demo)

Winter conditions can reduce range because cabin heating demand increases and battery performance can change with temperature. Preconditioning while plugged in can sometimes improve comfort without draining driving range as much.

### Maintenance differences vs petrol (high level)

EVs still need brakes, tyres, suspension checks, cabin filters, and software updates. Brake wear can be lower in some driving patterns due to regenerative braking, but tyres may still wear quickly if the vehicle is driven spiritedly.

### Academic framing

A dissertation can compare "stated WLTP-style figures" vs "user-reported real world" as a methodology section. This file supports that discussion without asserting proprietary test results.

### Safety note

Never infer charging cable compatibility without checking the vehicle's charging port standard for your region.
