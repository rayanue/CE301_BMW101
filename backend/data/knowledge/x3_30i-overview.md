## BMW X3 xDrive30i (demo overview)

The X3 is commonly described as a mid-size SUV that aims to blend practicality with a sporty feel. In coursework, it is a useful example for discussing AWD systems, ride height, and family-oriented packaging.

### Practical strengths (conceptual)

SUV shapes can make child seat loading and boot access easier for some buyers. AWD can be helpful in slippery conditions, though tyres are often the dominant factor in real grip.

### Trade-offs (demo)

Higher ride height can increase aerodynamic drag compared to a saloon, which can affect motorway fuel economy. Larger footprints can also affect urban parking ease.

### Buyer questions (demo)

Check tyre condition, alignment, and any reported vibration under load. SUVs can expose suspension wear more noticeably on rough roads.
